Welcome to the Land of Fire

Azerbaijan, often called the "Land of Fire," is a captivating country where East meets West, creating a unique blend of traditions, culture, and landscapes. Located at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, this hidden gem offers travelers an unforgettable experience filled with rich history, stunning architecture, delicious cuisine, and warm hospitality.

From the modern skyline of Baku with its iconic Flame Towers to the ancient rock art of Gobustan, Azerbaijan is a land of contrasts that will enchant every visitor. Whether you're seeking adventure in the Caucasus Mountains, relaxation on the shores of the Caspian Sea, or cultural immersion in historic villages, Azerbaijan has something for everyone.

Top Destinations

Explore the most breathtaking and culturally rich destinations Azerbaijan has to offer

Baku

The capital city blends ancient history with futuristic architecture. Visit the UNESCO-listed Old City (Icherisheher), admire the iconic Flame Towers, and stroll along the beautiful Baku Boulevard along the Caspian Sea.

Capital City

Old City (Icherisheher)

This UNESCO World Heritage site dates back to the 12th century. Explore the narrow streets, visit the Maiden Tower, Palace of the Shirvanshahs, and discover ancient mosques and bathhouses.

Baku

Gobustan National Park

Home to over 6,000 prehistoric rock carvings dating back 40,000 years. This UNESCO World Heritage site also features mud volcanoes and incredible natural landscapes.

60km from Baku

Sheki

A historic city on the Silk Road known for its Palace of Sheki Khans with intricate stained glass work. Famous for its sweets, crafts, and beautiful mountain setting.

Northwestern Azerbaijan

Gabala

A popular mountain resort destination with skiing in winter and lush forests in summer. Visit Tufandag Mountain Resort, Nohur Lake, and enjoy the scenic Seven Beauties Waterfall.

Northern Azerbaijan

Lankaran

A subtropical region known for its tea plantations, citrus orchards, and the amazing Hirkan National Park - home to the endangered Caucasian leopard and ancient boxwood trees.

Azerbaijan Through the Seasons

Azerbaijan is a year-round destination with each season offering unique experiences and breathtaking landscapes

Winter

Perfect for skiing at Shahdag and Tufandag resorts from December to March. Experience festive celebrations in Baku with Christmas markets and New Year events at Fountains Square.

Best for: Skiing, winter sports, and festive celebrations

Spring

Celebrate Novruz, the traditional new year festival in March. Enjoy blooming landscapes at Goygol and Maralgol lakes near Ganja. May is ideal for exploring historical sites in Sheki and Gabala.

Best for: Cultural festivals, nature exploration, and mild weather

Summer

Enjoy the beaches along the Absheron Peninsula and Caspian Sea from June to August. Experience major events like the Azerbaijan Grand Prix and Summer International Music Festival.

Best for: Beach vacations, major events, and countryside exploration

Autumn

Experience the grape harvest in wine regions like Gabala, Ismayilli, and Shamakhi. Enjoy milder temperatures perfect for hiking in national parks with spectacular fall foliage.

Best for: Wine tours, fall foliage, and mountain hiking

Culture & Cuisine

Discover Azerbaijan's rich cultural heritage and delicious traditional cuisine

Traditional Azerbaijani Cuisine

Azerbaijani cuisine is a delicious blend of influences from the Middle East, Mediterranean, and Central Asia. The food culture places great emphasis on hospitality, with meals meant to be shared and enjoyed together.

Plov (Pilaf)

The king of Azerbaijani cuisine - saffron-infused rice with various meats, dried fruits, and herbs.

Kebabs

Various skewered and grilled meats like lula (ground meat), tika (chunked meat), and basdirma (marinated meat).

Dolma

Stuffed grape leaves or vegetables filled with minced meat, rice, and herbs.

Qutab

Thin, crescent-shaped savory pancakes filled with meat, cheese, or greens.

Cultural Heritage

Azerbaijan's culture is a fascinating blend of influences shaped by its position at the crossroads of East and West. The country's heritage encompasses centuries of artistic traditions, music, dance, and craftsmanship.

Mugham Music

UNESCO-recognized traditional musical form with complex vocal improvisations.

Carpet Weaving

An ancient tradition with distinct regional patterns. Visit the Azerbaijan Carpet Museum in Baku.

Novruz Celebrations

Spring festival marking the new year with traditional foods, games, and customs.

Cultural Experiences

Traditional Music Performance

Experience a live Mugham performance at the International Mugham Center in Baku, or catch a folk music concert featuring the tar, kamancha, and other traditional instruments.

Carpet Weaving Workshop

Participate in a traditional carpet weaving workshop in Quba or Sheki regions, where you can learn about this ancient craft and create your own small piece to take home.

Cooking Class

Join a cooking class in Baku to learn how to prepare traditional Azerbaijani dishes like plov, dolma, or pakhlava under the guidance of a local chef.

Getting Around Azerbaijan

Transportation options to help you navigate through Azerbaijan with ease

Public Transportation

  • Baku Metro: Clean, affordable subway system covering major parts of the capital city.
  • City Buses: Extensive network in Baku and other major cities. Use BakiKart for payment.
  • Intercity Buses: Comfortable coaches connecting major cities and towns.

The most economical way to travel, especially in Baku where the metro system is efficient and well-maintained.

Taxis & Ride-sharing

  • London Cabs: Official purple taxis in Baku with meters.
  • Bolt and Uber: Popular ride-sharing apps operating in Baku.
  • Traditional Taxis: Available throughout the country, negotiate fare before riding.

Convenient and relatively affordable, especially when using ride-sharing apps which eliminate language barriers.

Railways

  • Modern Trains: Connect Baku to Ganja, Gabala, Sheki, and other cities.
  • Scenic Routes: Especially beautiful when traveling to the northwestern regions.
  • Comfort Classes: Options ranging from standard to business class.

A comfortable and scenic way to travel between major cities, with modern trains on many routes.

Car Rental

  • International Companies: Avis, Hertz, and Sixt operate in Baku.
  • Local Rentals: Often more affordable than international companies.
  • Requirements: International Driving Permit recommended.

Ideal for exploring remote areas and setting your own schedule, though navigating Baku traffic can be challenging.

Domestic Flights

  • Azerbaijan Airlines (AZAL): Operates domestic flights to Ganja, Nakhchivan, and Lankaran.
  • Time-saving: Particularly useful for reaching Nakhchivan, which is separated from main Azerbaijan territory.

Limited routes but can save significant time when traveling to distant regions of the country.

Tour Operators

  • Day Tours: Convenient options from Baku to popular destinations like Gobustan or Quba.
  • Multi-day Tours: Comprehensive packages covering multiple regions.
  • Private Guides: Available for hire with vehicles for customized experiences.

The most hassle-free option, particularly for those with limited time or who prefer having a knowledgeable guide.

Transportation Tips

BakiKart

Purchase a BakiKart for use on Baku's public transportation system. Available at metro stations and some convenience stores.

Road Conditions

Main highways are well-maintained, but rural roads may be in varying conditions. Consider a 4WD vehicle for remote mountain areas.

Train Tickets

Book train tickets in advance, especially during summer and holiday periods. Available online at ady.az or at train stations.

Taxis

Always negotiate the fare before getting in unofficial taxis. Using ride-sharing apps is recommended to avoid overcharging.

Essential Travel Tips

Make the most of your trip to Azerbaijan with these practical travel tips

Visa & Entry

  • Most visitors need an e-visa, obtainable through evisa.gov.az
  • Standard e-visa processing takes 3 business days
  • Passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned departure

Money & Costs

  • Currency: Azerbaijani Manat (AZN)
  • ATMs widely available in cities; credit cards accepted in most urban establishments
  • Carry cash for rural areas and small businesses

Language

  • Official language: Azerbaijani
  • Russian is widely spoken as a second language
  • English proficiency increasing, especially in tourism sector and among younger generations

Safety

  • Azerbaijan is generally very safe for tourists
  • Low crime rate, especially violent crime
  • Use standard precautions in crowded areas
  • Avoid border regions with Armenia

Internet & Connectivity

  • Good 4G coverage in urban areas
  • Free Wi-Fi in most hotels, cafes, and restaurants
  • Local SIM cards available at airport and mobile shops (passport required)

Etiquette & Culture

  • Azerbaijanis are known for hospitality and friendliness
  • Modest dress recommended when visiting mosques or rural areas
  • Remove shoes when entering homes if hosts do so
  • It's polite to try offered food and drink

When to Visit

Best Time to Visit

April to June and September to October offer the most pleasant temperatures and ideal conditions for exploring both cities and nature.

Summer (June-August) is perfect for beach vacations on the Caspian Sea, while winter (December-February) is ideal for skiing in the mountain resorts.

Key Festivals & Events

  • Novruz (March) - Traditional New Year celebration with special foods and customs
  • Baku Jazz Festival (October) - International jazz performances
  • Azerbaijan Grand Prix (April/May) - Formula 1 racing in Baku
  • Pomegranate Festival (October) - Celebration of Azerbaijan's beloved fruit in Goychay
