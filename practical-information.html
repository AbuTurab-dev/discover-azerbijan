Practical Travel Information

Essential tips and facts to help you plan your trip to Azerbaijan

Currency & Banking

Azerbaijani Manat (AZN)

The official currency of Azerbaijan is the Azerbaijani Manat (AZN). As of 2023, the exchange rate is approximately 1.7 AZN to 1 USD. Currency exchange offices are widely available in cities and at the airport.

Banking Tips

  • ATMs are widely available in cities and accept international cards
  • Credit cards (Visa, Mastercard) accepted in most hotels, restaurants and shops
  • Carry cash when traveling to rural areas

Approximate Costs

  • Meal at mid-range restaurant: 15-25 AZN
  • Taxi ride in Baku: 5-10 AZN
  • 3-star hotel: 80-120 AZN/night

Transportation

Public Transport

  • Baku Metro: 0.30 AZN per ride with BakiKart
  • City buses: 0.30 AZN with BakiKart

Taxis & Rental Cars

  • Popular apps: Bolt, Uber, Yandex.Taxi
  • Car rentals available at airport and city centers

Travel Tips

Safety & Etiquette

  • Azerbaijan is generally safe for tourists
  • Remove shoes when entering homes

Packing Essentials

  • Modest clothing for religious sites
  • Sunscreen for summer months

Emergency Contacts

Important Numbers

  • Ambulance: 103
  • Fire: 101
  • Police: 102

Tourist Assistance

  • Tourist Police: *9001
  • Ministry of Tourism: +994 12 498 10 90

Language & Communication

Official Language

The official language is Azerbaijani (Azeri), a Turkic language. Russian is widely spoken as a second language, especially among older generations. English proficiency is increasing, particularly among younger people and in the tourism sector.

Useful Phrases

  • Salam - Hello
  • Sağ olun - Thank you
  • Necəsəniz? - How are you?
  • Başa düşmürəm - I don't understand

Communication Tips

  • Many signs in Baku are in both Azerbaijani and English
  • Download a translation app for offline use
  • Learn basic Russian phrases for rural areas
