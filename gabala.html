Gabala

A beautiful mountain resort town with stunning nature and rich cultural heritage

Discover Gabala

Gabala, located in the foothills of the Greater Caucasus Mountains, is one of Azerbaijan's most popular tourist destinations. Known for its clean mountain air, lush forests, and stunning landscapes, Gabala offers a perfect blend of nature, adventure, and cultural experiences.

The region has a history dating back over 2000 years, having once been the capital of Caucasian Albania. Today, visitors can enjoy modern resorts alongside ancient historical sites, making Gabala a unique destination that combines the old and new.

Visitor Information

History & Culture

Gabala was the ancient capital of Caucasian Albania, an important historical region. Archaeological finds in the area date back to the 4th century BC. The region has been influenced by various civilizations including the Romans, Persians, and Arabs.

Today, Gabala preserves its rich cultural heritage while developing as a modern tourist destination. The local population maintains traditional crafts, music, and cuisine that visitors can experience.

Visitor Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Winter (December-March) for skiing at Tufandag
  • Summer (June-September) for hiking and outdoor activities

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Layered clothing for changing mountain weather
  • Camera for stunning landscapes

Getting There

By Car

About 3 hour drive from Baku via the Baku-Shamakhi-Gabala highway. Scenic route with mountain views.

By Bus

Regular buses and minibuses depart from Baku International Bus Terminal. Journey takes approximately 4 hours.
