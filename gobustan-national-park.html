Gobustan National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site with ancient rock carvings and unique mud volcanoes

Discover Ancient History

Gobustan National Park, located about 60km southwest of Baku, is one of Azerbaijan's most fascinating historical and geological sites. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is home to over 6,000 rock engravings dating back between 5,000-40,000 years, providing a remarkable window into prehistoric life.

The park covers an area of 537 hectares and features not only the petroglyphs but also mud volcanoes and gas-stones. The rock carvings depict scenes of hunting, dancing, animals, and rituals, offering insights into the lives of ancient inhabitants of the region.

Visitor Information

History & Significance

The rock carvings of Gobustan were discovered in the 1930s and have since been recognized as one of the most important collections of prehistoric art in the world. The petroglyphs provide evidence of human settlement in the region dating back to the Upper Paleolithic period.

In 2007, Gobustan was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site for its outstanding universal value in illustrating significant stages of human history through its rock art and cultural landscape.

Visitor Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (April-May) and Autumn (September-October) for pleasant temperatures
  • Early morning or late afternoon to avoid midday heat

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen)
  • Water and snacks

Getting There

By Car

About 1 hour drive from Baku via the Baku-Alat highway. Well-signposted route with parking available at the site.

By Tour

Many tour operators in Baku offer half-day or full-day tours to Gobustan, often combined with visits to the nearby mud volcanoes or Yanar Dag (Burning Mountain).
