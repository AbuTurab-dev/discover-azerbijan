Sheki

A historic Silk Road city known for its stunning architecture and sweet delicacies

Discover Sheki's Rich Heritage

Nestled in the foothills of the Greater Caucasus Mountains, Sheki is one of Azerbaijan's most picturesque cities with a history dating back over 2,500 years. As an important stop on the ancient Silk Road, Sheki developed into a center of crafts, trade, and culture.

Today, visitors are enchanted by Sheki's well-preserved historic center, traditional architecture, and famous sweet shops. The city's cool mountain climate makes it a perfect summer retreat from Baku's heat.

Key Attractions

Must-see sights in and around Sheki

Palace of Sheki Khans

This 18th-century summer palace is a masterpiece of Azerbaijani architecture, featuring stunning stained glass shebeke windows and intricate frescoes. A UNESCO World Heritage site.

Central Sheki

Sheki Fortress

The 18th-century fortress walls enclose the historic center of Sheki, including the Khan's Palace. Walk along the walls for panoramic views of the city.

Historic Center

Sheki Caravanserai

This beautifully restored 18th-century inn once hosted Silk Road merchants. Today it operates as a hotel, allowing visitors to experience historic accommodations.

Central Sheki

Travel Tips

Getting There

  • By Bus: Regular buses from Baku (5-6 hours) and other major cities
  • By Train: Overnight train from Baku (8 hours) with comfortable sleeping cars
  • By Car: 4-5 hour drive from Baku via scenic mountain roads

Local Specialties

  • Sheki Halva: Famous layered pastry with nuts and spices
  • Piti: Traditional lamb and chickpea stew cooked in clay pots
  • Sheki Wines: Try local varieties from surrounding vineyards
