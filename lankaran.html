Lankaran

A subtropical paradise with tea plantations, historical sites, and beautiful coastline

Discover Lankaran

Located in southern Azerbaijan near the Iranian border, Lankaran is known for its subtropical climate, tea plantations, and beautiful coastline. The region offers a unique blend of nature, history, and culture.

Lankaran has a rich history dating back centuries, with influences from Persian, Russian, and local Talysh cultures. The region is famous for its citrus fruits, tea, and distinctive cuisine.

Visitor Information

History & Culture

Lankaran has been an important regional center for centuries, with influences from Persian, Russian, and local Talysh cultures. The city was an important trading post on the Caspian Sea and has a distinct cultural identity.

The region is known for its unique Talysh culture, language, and traditions. Visitors can experience traditional music, dance, and crafts that are distinct to this part of Azerbaijan.

Visitor Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (April-May) for mild weather
  • Summer (June-August) for beach activities

What to Bring

  • Light clothing for warm weather
  • Swimwear for beach visits
  • Camera for beautiful landscapes

Getting There

By Car

About 4 hour drive from Baku via the Baku-Astara highway. Scenic route with mountain and coastal views.

By Bus

Regular buses and minibuses depart from Baku International Bus Terminal. Journey takes approximately 5 hours.
