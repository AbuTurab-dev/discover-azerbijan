Old City (Icherisheher)

The historic heart of Baku, a UNESCO World Heritage Site with ancient walls and rich history

Discover Old City

Icherisheher, the Old City of Baku, is a unique historical and architectural reserve dating back to the 12th century. Enclosed by well-preserved fortress walls, this ancient city contains numerous historical monuments including the iconic Maiden Tower and the Palace of the Shirvanshahs.

Walking through the narrow cobbled streets of Old City is like stepping back in time, with traditional houses, mosques, and bathhouses that have stood for centuries. Today, the area blends its rich heritage with modern cafes, restaurants, and artisan shops.

Visitor Information

History & Culture

The Old City has been continuously inhabited since at least the 12th century, though archaeological evidence suggests settlement dates back much earlier. The area flourished under the Shirvanshahs dynasty and became an important center of trade and culture along the Silk Road.

Today, the Old City preserves its medieval urban fabric while housing museums, artisan workshops, and traditional restaurants. The area was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2000 for its outstanding universal value.

Visitor Tips

Best Time to Visit

  • Spring (April-May) and Autumn (September-October) for pleasant weather
  • Early morning or late afternoon to avoid crowds

What to Bring

  • Comfortable walking shoes for cobbled streets
  • Camera for historic architecture
  • Light jacket for cooler evenings

Getting There

By Metro

Nearest station: Icherisheher (Old City) metro station, just outside the walls

On Foot

Easily walkable from most central Baku locations. Main entrance is through Gosha Gala Gapisi (Double Gates).
