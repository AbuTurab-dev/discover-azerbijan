Useful Azerbaijani Vocabulary

While many Azerbaijanis speak English, especially in tourist areas, knowing some basic Azerbaijani phrases will enhance your travel experience. Here are essential words and phrases to help you communicate during your visit.

Greetings & Basics

Hello Salam

(sah-LAHM)

Goodbye Sağ ol

(sah-ol) - Literally "be healthy"

Thank you Təşəkkür edirəm

(teh-shek-KOOR eh-dee-rem)

Yes/No Bəli/Xeyr

(BEH-lee / KHAYR)

Please Zəhmət olmasa

(zeh-MET ol-ma-sa)

Excuse me Bağışlayın

(bah-ghish-LAH-yin)

Directions & Transportation

Where is...? ...haradadır?

(ha-rah-DAH-duhr)

Left/Right Sol/Sağ

(sol / sah)

Straight ahead Düz

(dooz)

Taxi Taksi

(TAHK-see)

Bus Avtobus

(ahv-toh-BOOS)

Train station Dəmiryol vağzalı

(deh-meer-YOL vahz-AH-luh)

Food & Dining

Menu Menyu

(men-YOO)

Water Su

(soo)

Bread Çörək

(CHOH-rek)

Tea Çay

(chai)

Delicious Dadlı

(DAHD-luh)

Bill please Hesab, zəhmət olmasa

(heh-SAHB, zeh-MET ol-ma-sa)

Emergency Phrases

Help! Kömək edin!

(KOH-mek eh-DEEN)

I need a doctor Mənə həkim lazımdır

(meh-NEH heh-KEEM lah-ZUHM-duhr)

Police Polis

(poh-LEES)

I'm lost Mən itirmişəm

(men ee-TEER-mee-shem)

Pronunciation Tips

  • c is pronounced like "j" in "jam"
  • ç is pronounced like "ch" in "church"
  • ğ is a silent letter that lengthens the preceding vowel
  • ə is pronounced like "a" in "about"
  • x is pronounced like "ch" in Scottish "loch"
  • q is a guttural sound made at the back of the throat
